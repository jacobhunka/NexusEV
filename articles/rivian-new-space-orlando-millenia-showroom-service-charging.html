Rivian Is Building a 40,000 Sq Ft Showroom, Service Hub, and Charging Stop Right Off I-4 in Orlando

RivianTrackr  ·  Jose Castillo  ·  Apr 13, 2026

Rivian is expanding its Orlando footprint in a significant way. The company is constructing a new 40,000 square foot facility off Interstate 4 across from the Millenia Mall, combining a showroom, service center, and charging hub all under one roof. Jose Castillo at RivianTrackr stopped by the site, which is currently a cleared lot, and says construction is expected to go vertical later this year.

Orlando has been one of Rivian's strongest markets since the early days of R1 deliveries, and this new location is the brand's third footprint in the area. The original service site on Jetstream Drive was instrumental during the delivery ramp but outgrew its capacity fairly quickly. Last July, Rivian opened a second service center on Shader Road, which has earned a reputation for fast turnaround times and has been a convenient pickup point for new owners taking delivery.

The Millenia-area location is a step up in visibility. Positioned in the heart of Orlando's tourist corridor, it puts the Rivian brand in front of out-of-state visitors who may not have a Rivian Space back home. That kind of passive brand exposure in a high-traffic location is harder to manufacture than a targeted ad campaign.

There is also a deeper connection worth noting. Rivian's name comes from the Indian River Lagoon right here in Florida, which makes Central Florida something more than just another expansion market on a map. Jose Castillo, who runs RivianTrackr from the area, says he has had a front-row seat to how fast the local Rivian community has grown since those first deliveries.

More details on the facility are expected as construction picks up pace.
