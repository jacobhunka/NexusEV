Inside the Rivian R2: Upcycled Wood, a Linear Dash, and Design Built Around Adventure

Rivian  ·  Apr 21, 2026

Rivian published an official interior design walkthrough for the R2 ahead of deliveries, and it is worth watching if you are trying to understand the thinking behind the cabin rather than just the specs. Nature is the stated starting point. The design team describes the R2 as a more accessible version of what Rivian built with the R1 lineup, and that shows in how the interior choices were made. Upcycled birch from the furniture industry runs through the cabin, treated in a way that echoes mid-century modern furniture rather than the thin veneer strips you find in most cars. The way the grain and character of the wood are left visible is a deliberate departure from how automotive trim typically handles the material.

The material choices sit inside a broader philosophy. Rivian is positioning the R2 as something that carries the brand's identity into a lower price bracket without stripping out what makes the brand identifiable. The adventure focus shows up in how the team talks about what the car needs to do: on-road comfort, off-road capability, storage, and user experience are framed as equal priorities rather than a hierarchy. That is a different brief than most small SUVs, which tend to optimize for urban practicality and treat capability as a secondary selling point. The upcycled sourcing also fits the outdoor brand positioning without overstating it as a carbon claim.

The design walkthrough focuses specifically on the linear quality of the dashboard layout. Horizontal lines running across the interior are described as a deliberate choice to make the cabin feel wider than the vehicle's physical footprint. The team worked through early forms using paper models to understand how the shapes would read before committing to the final design. The result, they describe, is an interior where technology and physical capability feel balanced rather than one dominating the other. The video does not cover specific features or specifications, only the design intent behind the cabin's character and material selections.

Bottom line: This is a short official video covering design intent, not a full review. If you are considering the R2, it gives you a clearer read on what Rivian was trying to build into the cabin and whether that aligns with how you actually want to use the vehicle.
